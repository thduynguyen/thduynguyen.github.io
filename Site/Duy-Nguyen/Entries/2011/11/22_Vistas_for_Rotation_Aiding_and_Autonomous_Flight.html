Vistas for Rotation Aiding and Autonomous Straight Flight

 

Chris and I, together with Kyel, have realized Frank’s idea to use Vistas, our name for far-away features, for aiding rotation estimation on the drone.

As shown below, Vistas are detected from tracking SURF features and observe their scale changes. See our Fusion paper here for more details. (Also, see my related work at CVPR 2009)





Since they are far-away, we also used Vistas as directions for autonomous flight and exploration. The following video shows our first autonomous flight with vistas. It successfully passed through the narrow door at the end of the hallway!





And this is our full autonomous flight towards vistas with human-assisted turns at the hallway corners.




You can see the drone collides to the walls. We later tried to avoid lateral collisions with Wall-Floor Features. More details can be found in our workshop paper.


Update 2014-04: Scherer’s group at CMU recently used a very similar idea to enable obstacle avoidance on an ARDrone.
